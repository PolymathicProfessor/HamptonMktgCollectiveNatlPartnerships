As a member of the Hampton Marketing Collective, you have access to seven of the most prestigious professional organizations in marketing, retail, and fashion. Each relationship is managed by a dedicated HMC Liaison and Student Ambassador who connect you to competitions, conferences, scholarships, and career networks. The FSF relationship is faculty-advised — overseen directly by the HMC Academic Advisor with a previous FSF scholar serving as student coordinator.

AMA
American Marketing Association
World's largest marketing professional organization · Est. 1937 · 320+ collegiate chapters

The AMA is the premier community for marketing students and professionals worldwide. Membership connects you to a national network of 320+ collegiate chapters, exclusive competitions, career resources, and the AMA International Collegiate Conference — the largest gathering of undergraduate marketing students in the country.

Student Membership — $29/year
Includes access to your local AMA Collegiate Chapter, national competitions (fall online + spring in-person at ICC), webinars, Marketing News publication, AMAF scholarships, and the Alpha Mu Alpha national marketing honor society. Graduating members receive one free year of professional membership.
ICC Conference Case competition AMAF scholarships Career week Alpha Mu Alpha 320+ chapters
Managed by the HMC AMA Liaison + Student Ambassador
NRF
National Retail Federation
World's largest retail trade association · NRF Foundation student programs · 120+ university partners

The NRF Foundation connects students to the $3.9 trillion retail industry through scholarships, career programming, and the signature NRF Foundation Student Program in New York City — a four-day immersive experience with retail executives, recruiters, and career fair from the world's top brands.

Key Student Opportunities
NRF Student Association membership, Next Generation Scholarship, Ray Greenly Scholarship, Rising Stars Scholarship, and the University Challenge — a business case competition awarding over $70,000 in scholarships and travel stipends. Student Program in NYC (January) gives access to Retail's Big Show.
Retail's Big Show NYC $70K+ University Challenge Next Gen Scholarship Student Program NYC RISE Up credential Recruiter access
Managed by the HMC NRF Liaison + Student Ambassador
NSN
National Sales Network
Premier professional organization for diverse sales and marketing professionals · Est. 1992 · 16+ chapters nationwide

NSN is the leading professional organization for African Americans in sales and marketing, with over 30 years of connecting diverse talent to Fortune-level companies. Their student program is one of the most powerful career launch opportunities available to HMC members — fully funded and highly competitive.

Annual Student Conference — All Expenses Paid
The NSN Student Sales & Marketing Conference (September, Atlanta) is an invite-only, fully funded experience for top students from 25+ universities. NSN covers 100% of travel, hotel, meals, and registration for selected NSN Fellows. Includes workshops, mentorship, a career fair with on-site interviews, and a $10,000 pitch competition.
All-expenses-paid conference $10K pitch prize On-site interviews Fortune company access T-SET under-30 program NSN Fellows
Managed by the HMC NSN Liaison + Student Ambassador
BRAG
Black Retail Action Group
Preparing professionals and students of color for executive leadership in retail and fashion · Est. 1970

BRAG has been the definitive organization for professionals and students of color in retail and fashion for over 50 years. Through its internship programs, scholarships, college clubs, and annual Scholarship & Awards Gala, BRAG creates concrete pathways from campus to executive leadership in the retail industry.

Student Membership Benefits
Access to BRAG's Summer Internship Program — a comprehensive hands-on program placing students with retail and fashion companies, culminating in a case study competition. BRAG College Clubs, networking events, workshops, industry panels, mentorship, and access to the annual Scholarship & Awards Gala. Career connections to fashion and retail executives.
Summer internship Case study competition College club chapter Annual gala Scholarships Exec mentorship
Managed by the HMC BRAG Liaison + Student Ambassador
HFR
Harlem's Fashion Row — HBCU Fashion Forum
ICON360 nonprofit · Premier platform connecting HBCUs to the fashion industry · Est. 2007

Harlem's Fashion Row (HFR) and its nonprofit ICON360 are dedicated to creating equitable opportunities for Black designers and HBCU students in the fashion industry. Through its HBCU Fashion Summit and partnerships with brands like Nordstrom, Gap, Tiffany & Co., Sam's Club, and American Eagle, HFR connects HBCU students directly to industry leaders and brand opportunities.

HBCU Student Programs
The HBCU Fashion Summit brings HBCU students together with retail and fashion executives for panels, mentorship, and networking. ICON360's 360 Fashion Scholarship (in partnership with Nordstrom) awards HBCU students scholarships plus a semester-long 1:1 mentorship with fashion industry leaders. HFR also facilitates design competitions with brand partners offering cash prizes up to $75,000.
HBCU Fashion Summit 360 Scholarship (Nordstrom) ICON360 grants Brand design competitions Industry mentorship NYFW access
Managed by the HMC HFR Liaison + Student Ambassador
CFDA
Council of Fashion Designers of America
America's foremost fashion trade association · Est. 1962 · $5.9M+ in scholarships awarded since 1996

The CFDA is the most prestigious fashion design organization in the United States, representing 400+ of America's top womenswear, menswear, jewelry, and accessory designers. Through its annual CFDA Scholarship Fund, the organization discovers and invests in exceptional student design talent — awarding scholarships ranging from $25,000 to $75,000 to BFA and MFA students demonstrating innovation, sustainability, and boundary-pushing design. Past recipients include some of fashion's most recognized names.

CFDA Scholarship Fund — Annual Scholarships up to $75,000
The CFDA Design Scholar Award ($75,000) is the highest honor — recognizing a BFA student for innovation in sustainable design, new technologies, or boundary-pushing concepts. Additional scholarships include the Geoffrey Beene Award ($50,000, MFA), the CFDA Design Scholar Award for DEI ($50,000), the Veronica Beard Creative Futures Scholarship ($50,000 each, 4 recipients + paid NYC internship), the Carolina Herrera & CFDA Women in Design Scholarship ($40,000 + NYC internship), and the PVH Foundation x CFDA Scholarship ($25,000). Applications open annually in spring.
$75K Design Scholar Award $50K Geoffrey Beene Award $50K DEI Design Award Veronica Beard internship Carolina Herrera internship NYFW show access 415+ scholarships awarded
Managed by the HMC CFDA Liaison + Student Ambassador · Applications open each spring
FSF
Fashion Scholarship Fund Faculty-Advised
Virgil Abloh™ "Post-Modern" Scholar Program · Foremost fashion education nonprofit in the U.S. · Est. 1937

The FSF is the most prestigious scholarship program in fashion education. The Virgil Abloh™ "Post-Modern" Scholarship, established in 2020 by the late Virgil Abloh (creator of Off-White and Artistic Director of Louis Vuitton Men's), provides scholarships to Black and African American students through an annual case study competition. This relationship is faculty-advised at Hampton — overseen directly by the HMC Academic Advisor with a previous FSF scholar as student coordinator.

Virgil Abloh "Post-Modern" Scholar Program — $10,000+
Each scholar receives at least $10,000 in scholarship support, participation in the FSF Summer Scholar Series (10 weeks of master classes with fashion leaders), access to the annual FSF Gala in New York City, LVMH Maison tours, 1:1 industry mentorship, and talent acquisition events. The Chairman's Award offers an additional $25,000. 60 Post-Modern Scholars selected annually from FSF's 75 member schools including 14 HBCUs.
$10,000+ scholarship FSF Gala NYC Summer Scholar Series LVMH Maison tours $25K Chairman's Award Industry mentorship HBCU network (14 schools)
